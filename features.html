Objective

In the present day in Thailand, there has been a large surge in public interest over air quality monitoring due to a record year in air pollution from a variety of sources, such as metro and road construction, rice field burning and most importantly motor vehicles. Combined with several incidences of large amounts of dust particles and smog covering large metropolitan areas across the country and a lack of trust in official government pollution data, it became apparent that an independent source of online information for air quality would be greatly beneficial. Therefore, our team decided to create a platform for wireless collection and web-based presentation of air quality data.

  1. Create a source for air quality information that is accessible to the public.
  2. Create a platform for a wireless sensor network that can be extended and refactored easily.
